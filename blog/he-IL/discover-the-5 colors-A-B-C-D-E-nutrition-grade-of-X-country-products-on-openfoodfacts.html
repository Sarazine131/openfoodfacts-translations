Simplifying nutrition labeling on packaging to help consumers choose healthier foods is the goal of the five-color scoring system proposed by Professor Serge Hercberg of the French National Nutrition and Health Program.



In the same spirit as the appliance energy label, this system provides a rating of A to E (each associated with a color of green to red) which would be displayed voluntarily on the front of the products. . This system is part of the Health Bill of Marisol Touraine which will be debated in the National Assembly on March 31, 2015.


More fruits and vegetables, protein and fiber, fewer calories, sugar, saturated fat and salt.

„5 פירות וירקות ביום” , "Less fat, less sweet, less salty" : the notes of color try to synthesize these messages well known to the general public but not necessarily easy to apply when shopping .

In order to establish a food score, a nutritional score is first calculated that takes into account the content of elements found to be nutritionally favorable (fruits and vegetables, protein and fiber) and elements considered to be unfavorable. energy, simple sugars, saturated fatty acids, and sodium corresponding to salt). The green, yellow, orange, fuchsia and red color notes (A, B, C, D, E) correspond to different intervals of the nutritional score.

סימון להשוואת מוצרי מזון בזמן הקניות

Unlike other rating systems (such as recommended daily intake percentages), the score is independent of the portion indicated by the manufacturer, which is often different for each brand and is sometimes underestimated compared to the portion actually consumed.
Not linking the note to the size of the portion makes it possible to compare the notes of different products before choosing which one to buy.

We contacted Professor Hercberg at the end of September 2014 to ask him for the exact formula of the nutritional score and the thresholds for determining the 5-color notes A / B / C / D / E recommended in his report "Proposals for a new impetus of the policy public health nutrition as part of the National Health Strategy " .

הצוות של פרופסור הרצברג שלח לנו את נוסחת החישוב ואת כל הפרטים הנדרשים לחישוב הציון התזונתי כך שהצלחנו להחיל את הנוסחה על המוצרים שבמסד הנתונים השיתופי Open Food Facts. Scoring is possible for products for which a sufficiently complete nutrition table is shown on the packaging. To date, we have been able to determine the rating of more than 10,000 food products.

View nutrition notes on your smartphone in augmented reality

אנו מציגים דירוג תזונתי ודירוג בצבע עבור כל גיליון מוצר ב־Open Food Facts ואלו גם מופיעים ביישומונים שלנו ל־Android,‏ iPhone ול־Windows Phone.

While waiting for the possible adoption of the color note system, and its possible voluntary application by the manufacturers on the products, you can now scan the product barcodes with the Open Food Facts application to see the nutritional ratings.

On Android mobiles (and soon iPhone), you do not even need to scan the barcode: thanks to the visual recognition technology of the company Moodstocks , simply place your phone in front of a product to see its nutritional score , and that even without internet connection, which is very useful especially in stores.

משקל המילים, הזעזוע שבידיעה

מעבר למלחמות המילים, ארגון Open Food Facts פועל למען שקיפות ופתיחות הנתונים על מוצרי מזון. We are convinced that these data are data of public interest - they are too important to be kept closed - and that is why we started 3 years ago to build a collaborative, free and open database , in which more than 1,500 contributors added data from more than 35,000 products.

These data have very numerous applications: decryption of labels (additives, allergens, etc.), comparison and research of products, education ... a dozen reuses of Open Food Facts data have already been inventoried.

This data can test, evaluate and refine a nutritional scoring system as a whole by applying it to 10,000 products rather than to weaker samples or focusing on emblematic but sometimes anecdotal examples.

To this end, we have put in place a series of tools so that everyone can test the system of color notes of the National Nutrition and Health Program:

Display of the distribution of nutritional notes

The Open Food Facts website allows you to explore the database in different facets such as product categories, brands, labels, and now nutritional notes. It is now very easy to obtain the distribution of nutritional notes for all products or for a given brand or category, for example breakfast cereals, and to display the corresponding products of a certain grade.

סימונים תזונתיים לפי קטגוריית המוצר

It is interesting to compare the nutritional notes of the different categories of products. We can see the categories of the best rated products (fruits and vegetables, compotes) and the lowest rated (chocolate, sausage, mayonnaise).

You can also see which categories have products that have a wide variety of ratings. It is for these categories that the rating system is the most useful because it allows consumers to take into account the nutritional score in their choices, and it can encourage manufacturers to improve the formulation of their products for them get better grades.

מצד שני, הדירוג פחות שימושי לקטגוריות שבהן לכל המוצרים יש בערך את אותם הערכים התזונתיים כמו סוגי דבש שכמעט לכולם יש את הדירוג D. המערכת יכולה להשוות לקטגוריות דומות כגון ממרחים פסטה או ריבות או דבש .
