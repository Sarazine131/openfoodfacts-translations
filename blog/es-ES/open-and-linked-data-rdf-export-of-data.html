The data of Open Food Facts were already open and free (in open data as they say), and now they are also linked. Y sí, libre y vinculados a la vez!

Libre porque la licencia abierta permite que los datos sean utilizados por todos y para cualquier propósito, y vinculados porque los datos ahora están vinculados no solo entre sí, sino también a otros conjuntos de datos, a través de la base de datos DBPedia.

Let me rephrase that: there is now a large file that contains Open Food Facts data on products, their ingredients and their nutritional composition. The products are related to the ingredients they contain, and the ingredients (some to start) refer to their page on DBPedia (a database from Wikipedia articles). Este archivo no es para los humanos, sino para los programas. Es posible consultarlo, formular consultas como "cuáles son los productos cuyo ingrediente principal es el azúcar", y cruzarlo fácilmente con otros conjuntos de datos.

Thanks to this file, the OFF data is now part of what is called "Linked Data" or "the Web of Data" . Los datos son más fácilmente reutilizables.

Los detalles técnicos:

I took part in the Datalift Camp to which Charles Nepote of Datalift invited me, and it was my first time with the Datalift tool. We started to "play" with the OFF data to see how to express it in RDF.

The data on ingredients and nutrients are described by an ontology food that we are creating with François Scharffe of the Laboratory of Informatics, Robotics and Microelectronics of Montpellier LIRMM and Emmanuel Nauer of the Lorrain Laboratory of Research in Computer Science and its LORIA applications. La idea es utilizar el mismo vocabulario para describir una receta (como las utilizadas por el proyecto Taaable en la que Emmanuel participa), o un producto alimentario manufacturado.

The RDF export is here: https://world.openfoodfacts.org/data/en.openfoodfacts.org.products.rdf (in XML / RDF)

And it is referenced on The Data Hub to facilitate the discovery of the Open Food Facts dataset.

The ingredients are parsed from the list of ingredients in text, there are errors, the format of the lists vary a lot from one product to another. I am going to improve the parser, and in parallel contributors correct the spelling errors present in the lists.

The product and ingredient URLs currently return the HTML webpage, but I'm going to add the content negotiation to return the RDF.

François generó equivalencias entre los ingredientes de los Productos de Open Food Facts y los mismos ingredientes en DBPedia. They are indicated by the owl:sameAs property, for example:
 <food:name> Potassium sorbate </food:name>
<owl:sameAs rdf:resource="https://en.dbpedia.org/resource/Sorbate_of_potassium" />
</rdf:Description>

No hay ningún punto final SPARQL. You can instead load the RDF export into a tool like Datalift to execute SPARQL queries. As an example, here is one of my first queries (to find products whose main ingredient is sugar):
PREFIX food: <https://data.lirmm.fr/ontologies/food#>
SELECT?s?WHERE code {
   ?s <https://www.w3.org/1999/02/22-rdf-syntax-ns#type> food: FoodProduct.
   ?s food: containsIngredient?i.
   ?i alimento: alimento <https://world.openfoodfacts.org/ingredient/sugar> .
   ?i food: rank "1" OPTIONAL {?s food: code?code}.
}

This is of course a work in progress, there is still a lot of data missing in the RDF export (brands, categories etc.). Se añadirán gradualmente.
